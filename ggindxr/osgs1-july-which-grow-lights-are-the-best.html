Which Grow Lights Are the Best?

There are a plethora of different indoor gardening technologies on the market today. From hydroponics systems to indoor herb gardening systems, you will be able to find something that works for you. Hydroponics systems have rapidly grown in popularity over the past few years. Hydroponics has one clear advantage over its competitors: it is completely natural and does not require the toxic chemicals often used by traditional outdoor gardeners. If you are just starting out and haven't yet explored the vast possibilities of hydroponics indoors, below are some basic guidelines to get you started.

LED grow light vs. HPS or High Pressure Sodium: A popular debate among hydroponics enthusiasts is whether LED grow lights are truly the best choice, or if they simply replace HPS bulbs with "cool" (not too bright) fluorescent bulbs that produce much less heat but draw much less energy. The answer to this question really depends upon how much you plan to grow, and whether or not you are growing in a larger structure like a grow tent or a small balcony. For example, if you plan to keep your plants inside a relatively confined space like a bedroom, you will likely benefit most from using HPS or fluorescent bulbs.

Which Grow Lights Are the Best? - For indoor plants, most hydroponic systems use low-intensity, high-pressure sodium or potassium lamps. These types of lamps produce less heat than other common grow light types, which allows you to control the moisture content of your plants while maintaining overall plant density. Low-pressure sodium lamps are an excellent choice for hydroponic gardening. Some specialty hydroponic lights, such as the high-pressure sodium systems, also use a high-voltage lamp to produce even more heat for more efficient light delivery. Using special hydroponic equipment and lamps, these special lights can create a much more even canopy and more uniform plant growth.

For indoor plants, the most popular choice is LED grow light systems. These are an affordable and very reliable choice, and they are preferred by the majority of indoor gardeners. Using much less electricity than most conventional grow lights, the best LED grow lights are capable of producing a bright light for long durations and low temperatures, which make them excellent choices for growing a wide variety of delicate plants. However, the downside of LED grow lights is their price: they can sometimes be much more expensive to purchase initially, despite their low running costs over time, and so indoor gardeners on a budget might like to look for cheaper alternatives.

Indoor plants typically require different lighting levels, so a good starting guide for choosing which hydroponic lights are the best is to know the differences between the three primary types of hydroponics lighting systems available. The most popular are HID, or high-intensity discharge lights; they utilize small amounts of electricity but give out large amounts of light, which is ideal for growing small quantities of hydroponic vegetables and flowers. Cool lights, which use water pumps to circulate cool air over large areas of LED grow lights, provide good light coverage but require more electricity. Finally, blue lights, which are produced by fluorescent bulbs but spread out over a wider spectrum, are used to promote root growth in indoor environments. The spectrum that blue light provides is generally more efficient than either of the other two, so depending on your specific needs you might want to consider that hydroponic lighting system is best suited for your goals.

When considering which hydroponic lights to buy for a given plant, there are a few things to consider: if the plant likes full-spectrum light (UVA, UVB, and full spectrum lighting), then the LED grow lights are the best. If the plants do not like full spectrum light, then the compact fluorescent light is the best. If the plants are fast growing or seedlings, then the HPS or High Pressure Sodium grow lights are the best. Finally, the indoor plant housing chosen for your tubing lights is good for long-term solution and is usually quite sturdy to keep your lighting rig in place. With the proper setup, a hydroponics system can be built to last for several years. All that's left is to maintain it properly and to keep feeding the plants!
