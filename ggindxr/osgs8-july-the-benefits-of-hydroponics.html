The Benefits of Hydroponics

Hydroponics is simply a method of cultivating plants indoors using no soil at all. Rather than extracting mineral nutrients required for plant growth from the earth, hydroponics provides all of these nutrients to the plants through a nutrient delivery system at the roots. Hydroponics works very well in all sorts of growing situations from simply growing a tiny collection of herbs inside a kitchen all the way up to thousands of plants grown in a huge indoor greenhouse. Let's take a closer look at hydroponics, how it works, and some helpful tips for starting a hydroponics garden of your own.

Hydroponics systems are designed around the idea that there should be a constant supply of a certain nutrient solution to the roots of the plants. The nutrient solution can be replenished any day throughout the year, but there are several different types of hydroponics systems that are designed to fit different growing situations and each works a little differently. In the hydroponics industry, seasonal hydroponics systems are most often used. Generally, such systems will start out with one growing season where the nutrient solutions are replenished throughout the growing year. Such systems work best for traditional gardens that are not growing continuously year-round. However, such systems can be adapted to most any type of gardening endeavor, including those that are perennial, bi-annual, or year-round so you can fit multiple growing seasons into a single year.

There are four primary components needed in any hydroponics system: a source of nutrients, a reservoir of water, and a growing medium. When these four things are brought into play, hydroponics can easily support a huge variety of plants. A hydroponics system can support a single plant or thousands of them, depending on the type of system used and the size of the plants being grown. Hydroponics nutrients, which are concentrated nutrients dissolved in water, must meet specific needs in order to deliver the proper amounts of minerals to the roots of the plants being supported. Hydroponics nutrient solutions can be mixed in advance to deliver just the right type of nutrients to the plants depending on their growth stage.

Nutrients are delivered to the growing plants through the water supply system which is either a reservoir system or an electrical pump housed in grow tents. One nutrient, potassium, is the most important nutrient to hydroponics because it provides a source of energy for plant growth. Many other nutrients are also found in hydroponics, such as nitrogen, phosphorus, magnesium, and calcium. Plants get their energy from light, so they use light from the sun in order to produce food. Indoor gardens must have supplemental light as most indoor spaces do not give plants access to an optimal amount of light to ensure good growth.

Plants grown in hydroponics are usually classified into three groups: surface-based, submerged, and root-based. Plants that are based on the surface tend to be the most efficient at growing and are usually the most easily grown. Underwater hydroponics systems work with deep water reservoirs, while submerged systems are built below the surface and can either be above or below the water level. Root systems, also called rhizomes, are rooted in the growing media.

Hydroponics systems that have been built with water reservoirs are much easier to manage than those that rely on the plants being grown outside in the garden. It is much easier to move your indoor system to clean it and ensure good hygiene. Cleaning and pest control are easier with hydroponics because you can simply move the components to clean them whenever you feel it is necessary, and there are fewer places for pests to hide. Plants grown in hydroponics systems need less frequent attention since the nutrient solution levels are constantly monitored, and there is no need for time consuming weeding sessions which saves money and time.

Some of the things that can be grown with hydroponics are herb gardens, vegetables and flowers. Plants are typically started in a reservoir that contains water. There are many different reservoirs that can be used; there are plastic reservoirs, glass, steel or aluminum and the type of container does not matter as long as it contains enough water for the plants. Water circulation is very important to a successful hydroponics system, so aeration and water supply nozzles should be carefully selected so the water and air flows continuously over the plants.
